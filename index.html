Taste of a magical cup of Tea

For more than 200 years the name Ceylon Tea has been infamous for providing quality tea to the world. A symbol of greatness that has been carried down for many years since the introduction in 1867.

How it started

Tea was a non native plant in the island until it was brought down in 1867 from china and planted in the Royal Botnical garden in Peradeniya, Kandy. In 1875 the first shipment of ceylon tea was sent to the London tea auction.

  • Tea was introduced to Srilanka by British in 1867.
  • The first notable planter is Sir James Taylor.
  • Tea is one of the most popular exports out of Srilanka joining rubber and coconut.

Why so special

SriLankan tea has been a trademark beverage in many households from breakfast to evening relaxation around the world. Sri lankan tea has also known to contain antioxidents and amazing health benifits for the consumers.

  1. Srilankan tea is mostly popular in European and Middle eastern countries.
  2. Srilanka exports aproximately 300 million kgs of tea every year.
  3. Srilankan tea is rich in antioxidents and also linked to many health benifits like lowering the blood sugar levels.
